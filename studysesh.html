StudySesh

On Demand Tutoring

Introduction

During the 2020-2021 academic year, I participated in the illustrious pitch competition hosted by a nonprofit organization, Black Venture Capital Consortium(BVCC). My partner and I noticed there was a huge problem during the pandemic. There is currently no platform that provides college students with flexible on-demand access to specialized tutors. Our solution is StudySesh, an on-demand marketplace with a mission to help connect college students with tutors at any time of the day. For this project, my partner, Destiny Love, was the chief executive officer. I was the chief technical officer and we both were chief design officers. During this semester-long project, we decided who our customer is including their characteristics, drive, and why they fit, our marketing size, competitive landscape, value proposition, business model, and go-to-model strategy. This does not include the weekly sessions for each track(User Experience, Software Engineer, Venture Captial, and Entrepreneurship) with guest speakers for each track. I participated in not only software engineering but user experience too. This broaden my skills for future events. During this pitch competition program, my team participated in Demo Day 1 and 2, which were pitch competition practice rounds where we pitched our project in front of Venture Capital students, the lead of the entrepreneur track, and the founder of the nonprofit organization.

So in conclusion ...

In conclusion, even though my partner and I were not able to participate in the final round of the pitch competition we still improved a lot from the program. I was exposed to user experience concepts and tools as well as software engineer concepts and tools. Furthermore, I was able to practice and improve my public speaking and pitching skills.
